Vincent Van Gogh's tragic legacy

Start scrolling

Vincent Van Gogh was born on March 30, 1853, in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands, to a Dutch Reformed minister, Theodorus Van Gogh, and his wife, Anna Cornelia Carbentus. He has been painting since a young age.

"I'm seeking, I'm trying,
I'm in it with all my heart"

After completing his education, Vincent worked as an art dealer in London, England, where he became interested in social and humanitarian issues, such as poverty and illness, which would later influence his art.

Van Gogh was a deeply emotional person and his art reflected that. Despite facing rejection and financial struggles throughout his life, Vincent remained committed to his art. Art certainly was the most dearest thing to him.

"What would life be,
if we had no courage
to attempt anything"

Vincent's family moved frequently, and he attended several schools in different cities, In 1886, Vincent moved to Paris, France, to join his younger brother Theo, who was an art dealer. He studied at the École des Beaux-Arts and met artists like Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec and Paul Gauguin, who inspired him to experiment with new styles and techniques.

In 1888, Van Gogh moved to Arles in the south of France, where he found inspiration in the beautiful scenery and warm climate.

It was during this time that he painted some of his most famous works, including Starry night, Sunflowers and Café Terrace at Night. These paintings are still celebrated today for their vibrancy and unique style.

"when I have a terrible need
of - shall I say the word -
religion. Then I go out
and paint the stars"

Vincent's mental health began to decline in the late 1880s, and he was hospitalized several times for psychotic episodes, seizures, and anxiety attacks. He struggled with loneliness, poverty, and alcoholism, and had a falling out with Gauguin, who he had invited to stay with him.

In 1889, Vincent suffered a mental breakdown and cut off his left ear, either in an act of self-harm or as a gift to a prostitute. He was admitted to a hospital in Arles, where he continued to paint and correspond with his brother Theo. Despite treatments, his mental health condition was on a decline.

"Art is to console those who
are broken by life."

Vincent died on July 29, 1890, in Auvers-sur-Oise, France, at the age of 37. It is believed that he shot himself to death, but the real reason behind his death still remains controversial.

He is believed to have shot himself in the chest, but some theories suggest that he may have been shot by someone else.

His suicide note read:
"The sadness will last forever"

Despite the tragedy of his death, Van Gogh's legacy has endured. His paintings continue to captivate audiences around the world, and his influence on the world of art cannot be overstated.

His work has shaped the future of art for decades to come.
